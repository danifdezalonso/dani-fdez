I have studied the bachelor's degree in Information and Documentation. Nonetheless, I have always been involved in technology, specially in those issues related with Design.

The degree sharpened my analytical, organizational, collaborative and empathetic skills. Through my jobs I learned to be self-taugh, to listen and to provide me with a utilitarian mindset.

In Design I have not only found my passion but also the perfect playground to combine these and more skills and obtaining unexpected results!

Since I was a child, I have been into graphic design. I learned on my own thanks to online courses and this is how I got my first job.

I discovered the UX/UI Design world almost at the end of the degree, and since then, it has been my professional and academic passion. For my degree's final project I redesigned the website of the univerisity. Such project opened me the doors to my first job as UX/UI Designer.

Without knowing it, my life was divided in two branches of knowledge (research/documentation and design) until I found the UX/UI world, in which I merged both.

When I finished the degree I enrolled in a postgraduate course about Interaction and Usability Design at the UOC.

The following year, I juggled an official master's degree about UX Research in UNIR and a UX/UI Design bootcamp in IronHack while I kept working. And I survived! It has been a year of personal overcoming and immense apprenticeship.

I am now seeking to keep growing and learning as UX/UI Designer.
